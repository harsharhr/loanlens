What is Compound Interest?

Compound interest is interest calculated on both the initial principal and the accumulated interest from previous periods. Unlike simple interest (which only earns on the principal), compounding causes wealth to grow exponentially — Albert Einstein reportedly called it "the eighth wonder of the world."

The Formula

A = P (1 + r / n) nt

Nominal Rate vs Effective Annual Rate (EAR)

The nominal rate is what's advertised. The EAR is what you actually earn after compounding kicks in. Formula: EAR = (1 + r/n)n − 1. At 6% compounded monthly, EAR ≈ 6.17%.

Rule of 72

Divide 72 by your interest rate to estimate years to double. At 6%, money doubles in ≈ 12 years. This calculator shows the exact figure in "Time to Double" above.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does compound frequency affect my returns? +
More frequent compounding means interest is calculated and added more often — each time on a slightly larger balance. Daily vs annual compounding can add up to 0.5–1% more effective return per year at typical savings rates. Over 20+ years that gap is significant.
What is the difference between compound and simple interest? +
Simple interest: Interest = P × r × t — always on the original principal. Compound interest earns interest ON interest. For $10,000 at 5% over 20 years: simple interest gives $20,000; monthly compounding gives $27,127 — a $7,127 difference purely from compounding.
Can I use this for SIP or recurring deposits? +
Yes. Select "Deposits" and set the frequency to monthly. For SIP (Systematic Investment Plan), set compound frequency to monthly, and add your monthly SIP as a regular deposit. The calculator models your projected corpus accurately.
What does "deposit timing" mean? +
Beginning of period means your deposit earns interest during the current period. End of period means the deposit is added after interest for the period is applied. Beginning-of-period deposits earn slightly more because they compound for one extra period.
Is this accurate for bank FDs in India? +
Yes — set compound frequency to Quarterly (standard for most Indian banks), select ₹ as currency, and enter your FD amount. Note that TDS on FD interest is not factored in. For RD, switch to Deposits mode with monthly frequency and set initial investment to 0.
How is the effective annual rate (EAR) calculated? +
EAR = (1 + r/n)^n − 1, where r is the nominal annual rate and n is the number of compounding periods per year. Example: 10% nominal, compounded monthly → EAR = (1 + 0.10/12)^12 − 1 ≈ 10.47%. This is the true annual rate you actually earn.
